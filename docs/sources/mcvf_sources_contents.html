Agnes

Filename: 122x-agnes-mcvf-p
Edition: Taylor, Robert, ed. 1969. Sermon anonyme sur sainte Agnès, texte du XIIIe siècle. Travaux de linguistique et de littérature de l'Université de Strasbourg 7:241-253.
Date of composition: c1200 (BFM), 1st half 13th c. (Taylor 1969:241)
Base manuscript: Nantes, Musée Dobrée, Ms. 5, fol. 223-229 (Taylor 1969:241)
Date of manuscript: ca. 1200
Notes: The print edition is also the basis for BFM's online edition, SAgnesDob. Based on the third chapter of a Latin life of Agnes (Gesta sanctae Agnetis) and 3 Kings 1, 1-5. Independent of other translations of the Gesta into French (Taylor 1969:242). For information about mss., see edition.
Number of words: 4,691

Aucassin et Nicolette

Filename: 122x-aucassin-mcvf-m
Edition: Roques, Mario, ed. 1936. Aucassin et Nicolette: Chantefable du XIIIe siècle. Paris: Champion.
Date of composition: last quarter 12th c. or 1st half 13 c. (BFM)
Base manuscript: Unnamed manuscript
Date of manuscript: end 13th c. (BFM), "de la derniere parti du XIIIe siècle" (Roques 1936:xiii)
Notes: The print edition is also the basis for BFM's online edition, aucassin. The text alternates between vers assonant and prose. The IDs for the individual sentence tokens indicate the genre for that token.
Number of words: 10,201

Brendan

Filename: 1120-brendan-mcvf-r
Edition: Short, Ian and Brian Merrilees, eds. 1979. The Anglo-Norman voyage of St Brendan. Manchester: Manchester University Press. Online version.
Date of composition: between 1106 and 1121 (ARTFL)
Base manuscript: London, British Library, Cotton Vespasian B. X (I), ff. 1-11
Date of manuscript: Meyer 1906:341 dates the work to "probablement peu après 1121, date du mariage de cette reine" (Aélis, wife of Henry I of England).
Notes: Five other manuscripts exist. See Short and Merrilees 1979:6-8 for details. The form is octosyllabic rhyming couplets (Short and Merrilees 1979:8).
Number of words: 11,017

Pseudo-Turpin (MCVF sample)

Filename: 122x-pseudo-turpin-mcvf-p
Edition: Auracher, Theodor, ed. 1877. Der sogenannte poitevinische Pseudo-Turpin. Nach den Handschriften mitgetheilt. Zeitschrift für romanische Philologie 1:259-336.
Date of composition: c122x
Base manuscript: "Pariser Handschriften 5714 and 124" (Auracher 1877:259)
Date of manuscript: "wohl unzweifelhaft aus dem dreizehnten Jahrhundert" (Auracher 1877:260)
Notes: The MCVF sample of the text (pp. 262-272, line 17) is supplemented by the Penn sample (pp. 272, line 18-336), which contains the remainder of the text and thus yields an exhaustive sample. The text was translated from Latin c1200. The original translation is lost, but was recopied three times about 50 years later (C. Meredith-Jones, The chronicle of Turpin in Saintonge. http://www.jstor.org/stable/2848398). There is a printed edition 1527, not identical to the earlier manuscripts.
Number of words: 3,805
