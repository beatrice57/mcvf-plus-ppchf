Somme, Royal

Filename: 1279-somme-royal-penn-p
Edition: Tysor, Ann Brooks, ed. 1949. Somme des vices et des vertus. M.A. thesis, University of North Carolina. 1-85.
Date of composition: 1279
Base manuscript: British Museum, British Royal Manuscript Ms. 19cII (photostatic copy: Washington, DC, Library of Congress, MLA Rotograph collection, no. 306) (Tysor 1949:viii)
Date of manuscript: not available
Notes: Tysor 1949 contains the first part of the Somme. The second part, also based on the Royal manuscript, is published in Allen 1951.
We thank Suzanne Evans Wagner for providing us with photocopies of both dissertations, the raw text that we parsed, and the following information concerning two further important manuscripts. The first, MS Mazarine 870, is published in Brayer and Leurquin-Labie 2008, a fully revised version of Brayer's 1940 doctoral dissertation, which in addition to the transcription includes a glossary, a helpful introduction, and notes on the text. The second manuscript is British Museum, Cotton Cleopatra A.V, Egerton, 945, Add., 24125, 28162 (Tysor 1949:viii). It is this manuscript that likely served as the basis for a translation into Middle English known as the Ayenbite of Inwyt. The translation is included in the Penn Parsed Corpus of Middle English, second edition. A draft transcription of the Cotton manuscript exists at Penn, but has not yet been parsed. Users wishing to compare the Ayenbite to the Somme should realize that unfortunately, the Royal manuscript is not as close to the Cotton as is the Mazarine.
