Annotation manual for historical French

Citation:

Diaconescu, Rodica. 2007. Part-of-speech annotation manual for historical French. https://www.ling.upenn.edu/~beatrice/annotation-french/pos.

Santorini, Beatrice. 2021. Syntactic annotation manual for historical French. https://www.ling.upenn.edu/~beatrice/annotation-french.

This manual is indebted to the morphosyntactic annotation manual for the Penn Parsed Corpora of Historical English (PPCHE). The present guidelines for historical French supplement the PPCHE guidelines where necessary. In cases of disagreement, the guidelines for French prevail for the French corpora. It is worth noting that for comparative and degree constructions, the present guidelines are more rational, and they will be adopted for English in the next release of the PPCHE.

The guidelines for the part-of-speech (POS) annotation were written by Rodica Diaconescu at the University of Ottawa in French. The parts in English were added by Beatrice Santorini at the University of Pennsylvania.

The guidelines apply to the following corpora:

  • MCVF Corpus, version 1.0, 2009
  • MCVF Corpus, version 2.0, 2021
  • Penn-BFM Parsed Corpus of Historical French, version 1.0, 2021
